Career Jobs and Opportunities

Bachelor of Science in Information Technology

 

Software Engineer
A software engineer 's job involves the design and programming of software at the system level, including operating systems , database systems and embedded systems.
They know how both software and hardware work. In the electronics and telecommunications industries, software engineers are also found. 
For a software engineering profession, a computing, software engineering or related degree is required.

Systems Analyst
In order to increase business performance and effectiveness, systems analysts use computer systems to investigate and analyze business issues and then develop information systems that offer a realistic solution, 
usually in response to demands from their organization or a client. For analyst programmers, 
it can be a job to step into and usually requires a few years of experience after graduation.

Business Analyst
Business analysts find opportunities for the use of information technology to enhance processes and business operations.
The task is project-based and starts with the examination of the requirements of a client, the selection and documentation of requirements and the creation of a project plan to design the resulting technology solution. 
To become a business analyst, you will need an understanding of technology, but you will not necessarily need a professional degree.

Technical Support
These are the IT world's trained trouble shooters. They track and maintain an organisation's information systems and networks.
Organisations depend tremendously on technical support officers. Some support lines involve professionals with specific expertise and skills, but for graduates, technical support may also be a good way into the industry.

Network Engineer
One of the most technically challenging IT jobs is network engineering. The function includes the implementation, maintenance, support, creation and design 
of communication networks within or between organizations within an organization. Protection, 
information storage and disaster recovery techniques are also the responsibility of network engineers. A degree related to telecoms or computer science is required.

Technical Consultant
In order to develop and execute IT programs for external customers, professional consultants have technical expertise.
At any or all phases of the project lifecycle, they might be involved: pitching for a contract; refining a customer team specification, designing the system, 
handling part or all of the project after sales support and even writing the code. For this position, a professional degree would be helpful, but not always required.

Technical Sales Engineer
In technology and distribution, technical sales engineers provide guidance and support for a variety of products.
You may sell hardware or praise the business advantages of entire systems or services. The work could include regular phone calls, meetings ,
conferences and proposals for drafting. A degree in technology is not necessarily necessary, but you would need to have a detailed technical knowledge of the product you are selling.


Project Manager
Project managers schedule and coordinate resources and individuals to ensure that tasks are completed on time, 
remain within budget and fulfill the expectations of the organization. From start to finish, they can manage a whole project or manage part of a larger 'programme'.
It is not an entry-level job and requires experience and a strong base of soft skills and technology that are necessary for working with tech development teams and company managers at higher levels.


Web Developer
Web development is a broad concept that encompasses everything to do with website construction and all of the infrastructure behind it.
The jobs can range from designing an intranet for workers to developing an online retailer's payment system.
Most web developers have IT skills and experience, but you can start immediately after university as a junior web developer and work your way up.

Software Tester
The quality assurance stage of software development and implementation is taken care of by a software tester. 
To ensure that the software produced by developers is fit for purpose, they perform manual checks. 
In order to predict pitfalls until work starts, testers may often be engaged in the early stages of ventures. 
The programming and use of coding languages would be familiar to many software testers. The majority of software testers will have a computer science or IT degree.

Bachelor of Science in Electronics Engineering

 

•Electronic Engineer
Electronics engineers create, design and develop everyday devices such as mobile phones, portable music devices and computers. 
Electronic engineering offers the chance to produce new innovations and developments in telecommunications, robotics, computing hardware, and power and electrical equipment.

•Field Tester Engineer
Develop standard procedures for field testing tasks. Evaluate operational performance and recommend improvements if needed.
Test the prototype product to determine its acceptability and recommend modifications if required. Conduct the product tests to predict performance or maintenance issues.

•Network Planning Engineer
The Network Planning Engineer is a professional who plays a significant role in the maintenance of an internal and external network of an organization. 

•Electronics and Communications Consultant
Electronics Engineers design, develop, adapt, install, test and maintain electronic components,
circuits and systems used for computer systems, communication systems, entertainment, transport and other industrial applications.

•Customer Support Engineer
Customer support engineers serve on the front lines by answering questions and troubleshooting technical problems through phone, email, and chat. They assist customers with onboarding of a company's product or service and also guide clients through installation and updating issues as needed.

•Electronics Technician
Electrical and electronics engineering technicians help electrical and electronics engineers design and develop computers,
communications equipment, medical monitoring devices, navigational equipment, and other electrical and electronic equipment.

•Service Engineer
The service engineer job description is to service the various machinery used in large industries. 
They are also known as field service engineers or even maintenance engineers. They analyze and service equipment machinery that is worn out. Machinery is made of iron or other such particles.

•Senior Sales Manager
The Senior Sales Manager leads and manages all sales operations and oversees activities of the junior sales management team.
The key objective of the Senior Sales Manager is to grow incremental and new sales for the business while simultaneously reducing customer turnover.

•Technical Director
The main duty of a Technical Director is to provide technical direction on business decisions and execution of specific projects. 
They primarily work with the sound, lighting and visual production process, and they typically have the following daily responsibilities: Overseeing the audio and visual production process.
