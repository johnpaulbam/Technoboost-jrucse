Mission

Vision

JRU will be a market leader in the use of technology for innovations in teaching and learning to produce graduates of social importance.

History of CSE

The College of Computer Studies and Engineering began as a computer science college. In order to meet the demands of a knowledge-based society and industry of the 21st century, computer studies and engineering provide adequate information and expertise to educate students locally and globally. Following government approval in 1995, the Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) program was offered. José Rizal University Computer Studies College. 2014, February. Frequently Asked Question

 

Are there Events/Activities Concerned with the College of Computer Studies and Engineering(CSE)?

YES, in fact, "CSE WEEK" was done in February 2020 wherein they held activities such as; IT Quiz Bee, e-Game tournaments, and Programming seminars.

Are the Computer Labs fully equipped for any student needs?

Besides Games, YES, as our systems have both the licensed products of Adobe Suite and Microsoft 365.

Are there existing Clubs in the College of Computed Studies and Engineering?

YES, there is one, "COMSOC" is an abbreviation for Computer Society as they are the ones responsible for the events as well as to answer inquiries related to CSE.

Do Graduates of BSIT and related courses take Board Exams?

The BS in Information Technology does not have a board examination. However, graduates may opt to take the Civil Service Examination (CSE) conducted by the Philippine Civil Service Commission (PCSC) to qualify in working in government offices.
